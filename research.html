research

Trained as both a lawyer and data scientist, I apply advanced data analysis (in R and Python), theoretical models, and experimental methods from the social sciences to address legal questions.

Topics: My research interests span several substantive fields of law: privacy, AI, information law, antidiscrimination law, and legal philosophy.

Methods: I integrate experimental designs with causal inference, machine learning, and text analysis to leverage non-traditional data sources such as images and texts. I follow the principle that converging evidence from multiple methods strengthens empirical legal analysis. Furthermore, I am interested in the epistemic relevance of empirical knowledge for normative legal reasoning. The objectivity of jurisprudence has long been a subject of debate. Law is not a pure science, but a battleground of values. Empirical research can enhance legal reasoning's objectivity, if the truly relevant facts are identified.
